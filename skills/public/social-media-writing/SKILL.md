# Social Media Writing Skill

## 1. Overview

This skill governs all social media copy creation across LinkedIn, X (Twitter),
TikTok, and Facebook. It provides the writer and optimizer agents with a
systematic methodology for producing high-converting, platform-native content
that sounds human, drives engagement, and avoids corporate clichés.

Activate this skill whenever the pipeline generates post text — from first
draft through final optimization.

## 2. Core Capabilities

- Platform-native tone calibration (professional, casual, energetic, warm)
- Post anatomy construction (hook → body → CTA)
- Character-count optimization per platform
- Formatting rules (whitespace, line breaks, emoji placement)
- Voice consistency across all posts in a weekly batch
- Anti-corporate language filtering
- Authenticity pattern enforcement
- Multi-format writing (text, carousel captions, thread tweets, video scripts)

## 3. When to Use This Skill

|---------|--------|

## 4. Detailed Workflow

### Step 1: Understand the Brief

Before writing a single word, extract from state:
- **Platform** → determines tone, length, formatting
- **Theme** → vision, product, engagement, or education
- **Hook idea** → from strategist (use as inspiration, not literally)
- **Product spotlight** → if theme is product, weave in naturally
- **Day of week** → affects tone (Monday = motivational, Friday = casual)

### Step 2: Set Tone Level

Use a 1-5 tone scale calibrated per platform:

| Level | Label | When to Use | Example Opener |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | Formal professional | Industry reports, partnerships | "New research from our team reveals..." |
| 2 | Conversational professional | LinkedIn thought leadership | "Here's what I learned building..." |
| 3 | Casual expert | X threads, education posts | "Quick thread on something I keep seeing..." |
| 4 | Energetic casual | TikTok, engagement posts | "OK so this is wild — " |
| 5 | Playful/bold | Memes, hot takes, viral attempts | "Delete your entire content strategy." |

**Default tone by platform:**
- LinkedIn: Level 2-3
- X: Level 3-4
- TikTok: Level 4-5
- Facebook: Level 2-3

### Step 3: Draft the Post Anatomy

Every post follows this three-part structure:

**Part A: The Hook (first line)**
- Must stop the scroll (see viral-hooks skill for frameworks)
- Must fit "above the fold" on the target platform
- Must work in complete isolation (shareable as a standalone quote)

**Part B: The Body (middle)**
- One idea per post — never cram multiple messages
- Show, don't tell: replace claims with evidence
  - Bad: "Our product is fast"
  - Good: "We went from 12-second load times to 0.3 seconds"
- Use short paragraphs (1-2 sentences on LinkedIn, 1 sentence on X)
- Include a "proof point" — a number, example, or mini-story
- For product posts, weave the product naturally into a story or insight

**Part C: The CTA (closing)**
- Every post needs a reason for the reader to act
- Never beg ("Please like and share!")
- Match CTA to objective:
  - Awareness → "Follow for more [topic]"
  - Engagement → "What's your take? Drop it below."
  - Traffic → "Link in comments" (LinkedIn) or "Link in bio" (TikTok)
  - Community → "Tag someone who needs this"

### Step 4: Apply Platform Formatting

**LinkedIn Formatting:**
```
[Hook line — bold or surprising]

[Blank line for dramatic pause]

[Body paragraph 1 — 1-2 sentences]

[Body paragraph 2 — evidence or example]

[Body paragraph 3 — insight or lesson]

[CTA — question or invitation]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```
- Short paragraphs separated by blank lines
- No links in the post body (algorithm penalty) — link goes in first comment
- Close with a question to drive comments
- 3-5 hashtags at the end, after whitespace

**X Formatting:**
```
[Hook — front-loaded value, under 100 chars]

[Body — compressed insight, every word earns its place]

[CTA — bookmarks, RTs, or follows]

#tag1 #tag2
```
- 280 characters max per tweet
- If content exceeds 280 chars, suggest a thread format
- 1-3 hashtags, inline or at end
- No unnecessary filler words

**TikTok Caption Formatting:**
```
[Hook — curiosity gap, under 80 chars] 🔥

[Body — 1-2 lines max, supports the video]

[CTA — save, follow, comment trigger]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3 #hashtag4 #trending
```
- Optimal caption length: 100-150 characters
- Max 2200 characters
- Emojis as visual bullet points (strategic, not excessive)
- 5-8 hashtags, mix trending and niche
- Caption complements video, doesn't repeat it

**Facebook Formatting:**
```
[Hook — relatable question or story opener]

[Body — warm, community-focused, 40-80 words]

[CTA — sharing, tagging, or commenting prompt]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2
```
- Optimal length: 40-80 words
- 2-4 hashtags at end
- Questions drive highest engagement on Facebook
- Tag-a-friend CTAs still work well

### Step 5: Voice Consistency Check

All posts in a weekly batch must share these voice traits:

| Trait | Description | Test |
|-------|-------------|------|
| Tech-savvy | Uses current terminology accurately | Would a developer nod along? |
| Accessible | No jargon without context | Would a non-tech person understand? |
| Concrete | Specifics over abstractions | Are there numbers, examples, names? |
| Builder voice | Sounds like someone who ships code | Does it feel like a founder wrote it? |
| Honest | No exaggeration or hype | Could you defend every claim? |

### Step 6: Anti-Pattern Filter

Run every draft through this filter. If any match, rewrite.

**Banned openers:**
- "Excited to announce..."
- "Thrilled to share..."
- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..."
- "I'm proud to..."
- "We're pleased to..."
- "As we navigate the complexities of..."

**Banned phrases:**
- "Synergy", "leverage", "disrupt", "game-changer", "revolutionary"
- "Best-in-class", "cutting-edge", "world-class", "seamless"
- "Innovative solutions for modern challenges"
- "Unlock your potential", "take it to the next level"

**Banned patterns:**
- Emoji overload (max 3 per post, 0 for LinkedIn professional posts)
- Hashtag stuffing (exceeding platform limits)
- Generic motivational quotes without original insight
- Links in LinkedIn post body
- Wall of text without line breaks

### Step 7: Final Polish

Before submitting the draft:
1. Read it out loud — if it sounds robotic, rewrite
2. Count characters — verify platform compliance
3. Check the hook — does it stop the scroll?
4. Check the CTA — does it give a reason to engage?
5. Check the proof point — is there at least one specific example?
6. Check voice — does it sound like the same person across all posts?

## 5. Frameworks and Models

### The POST Framework (for every piece of content)

| Letter | Stands For | Description |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| P | Platform | Which platform? Determines everything else |
| O | Objective | Awareness, engagement, traffic, or conversion? |
| S | Story | What's the one idea, insight, or narrative? |
| T | Trigger | What action should the reader take? |

### The AIDA Micro-Model (compressed for social)

| Stage | Social Adaptation | Length |
|-------|------------------|--------|
| Attention | The hook line | 1 line |
| Interest | The proof point or story | 2-3 lines |
| Desire | The "so what" — why this matters to the reader | 1-2 lines |
| Action | The CTA | 1 line |

### Character Limits Reference

| Platform | Hard Max | Optimal Range | Hook Budget |
|----------|----------|---------------|-------------|
| LinkedIn | 3000 chars | 1300-1800 chars | 210 chars (above fold) |
| X | 280 chars | 200-260 chars | 60-70 chars (scan zone) |
| TikTok | 2200 chars | 100-150 chars | 55-65 chars (preview) |
| Facebook | 63,206 chars | 40-80 words | 125 chars (mobile fold) |

## 6. Platform-Specific Guidelines

### 6.1 LinkedIn Deep Dive

**Audience mindset:** Professional growth, industry insights, career development.
People browse LinkedIn during work hours looking for content that makes them
smarter or more connected.

**What performs:**
- Personal stories with professional lessons
- Contrarian takes on industry norms
- Specific results with transparent methodology
- "Here's what I learned" frameworks
- Clean formatting with generous whitespace

**What fails:**
- Press releases disguised as posts
- Corporate announcements without human angle
- Posts that read like marketing copy
- Excessive self-promotion without value

**LinkedIn algorithm signals:**
- Dwell time (long reads with good formatting)
- Comments (especially conversations, not just emojis)
- Saves (content worth revisiting)
- Shares with added commentary

### 6.2 X Deep Dive

**Audience mindset:** Quick insights, sharp opinions, real-time commentary.
Users scroll fast and reward conciseness and wit.

**What performs:**
- Bold, concise statements (every word earns its place)
- Threads with strong standalone openers
- Numbers and specifics compressed tightly
- Quote tweets with sharp commentary
- Timely hot takes on industry news

**What fails:**
- Long-winded tweets that could be shorter
- Thread openers that only make sense with context
- Generic platitudes and motivational quotes
- Posting without engaging in replies

### 6.3 TikTok Deep Dive

**Audience mindset:** Entertainment first, education second. Users are in
discovery mode, swiping through content rapidly.

**What performs:**
- Curiosity gap captions that drive watch-through
- "Save this" educational content
- Behind-the-scenes authenticity
- Trend-riding with brand-relevant angles
- Searchable keyword phrases in captions

**What fails:**
- Corporate tone in captions
- Long paragraphs nobody reads
- Hashtags that aren't relevant to content
- Captions that don't match video energy

### 6.4 Facebook Deep Dive

**Audience mindset:** Community connection, personal stories, group belonging.
Users browse during downtime looking for relatable content.

**What performs:**
- Questions that invite personal stories
- Community-oriented language
- Warm, conversational tone
- Tag-a-friend and share prompts
- Longer storytelling (40-80 words)

**What fails:**
- Professional/LinkedIn-style tone
- Impersonal, third-person copy
- Sales-forward content without value
- Ignoring comments after posting

## 7. Quality Checklist

- [ ] Post follows the hook → body → CTA structure
- [ ] Hook fits above the fold on the target platform
- [ ] Body contains at least one proof point (number, example, story)
- [ ] CTA matches the post's objective

- [ ] Formatting follows platform-specific rules (whitespace, line breaks)
- [ ] No banned openers, phrases, or patterns detected
- [ ] Tone level is appropriate for the platform and content type
- [ ] Post sounds human when read aloud

- [ ] Hashtags follow platform limits (see hashtag-strategy skill)

## 10. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Better Approach |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| "Excited to announce" opener | Zero hook value, immediately skippable | Lead with the result or insight |
| Cramming 3 ideas into one post | Dilutes impact, confuses the reader | One idea per post, always |
| No proof point | Claims without evidence feel empty | Include one number, example, or story |
| Same tone across all platforms | Sounds inauthentic on platforms with different cultures | Calibrate tone per platform (see Step 2) |
| Wall of text on LinkedIn | Nobody reads paragraphs in a feed | Short paragraphs, generous whitespace |
| Links in LinkedIn body | Algorithm deprioritizes external links | Put links in the first comment |
| Emoji overload | Looks unprofessional, distracts from message | Max 3 per post, strategic placement |
| No CTA | Wasted engagement opportunity | Always give the reader a next action |
| Buzzword soup | Sounds like a press release, not a person | Replace every buzzword with a specific |
| Hashtag stuffing | Looks spammy, reduces credibility | Follow platform limits strictly |
| Copying hook literally | Strategist's hook idea is inspiration, not copy | Use the hook idea as a starting direction |
| Ignoring character limits | Truncated posts look broken | Check counts before finalizing |

## 11. Advanced Techniques

### The Callback Pattern
Reference a previous post to build narrative continuity:
"Last week I posted about our 45-minute query problem. 200 of you shared your
own horror stories. Here's what I did about it."

### The Pattern Interrupt
Break the expected format to stand out:
- Start with a single word: "No."
- Use a dramatic line break after 3 words
- Open with a quote from someone unexpected

### The Specificity Ladder
Each draft revision should get more specific:
- Draft 1: "We improved performance significantly"
- Draft 2: "We improved query speed by 95%"
- Draft 3: "We went from 45-minute Looker reports to 1.2-second responses"

### The Empathy Bridge (for Product Posts)
Never lead with the product. Lead with the problem:
1. Name the pain (the reader nods along)
2. Show you understand it deeply (build trust)
3. Reveal what you built to solve it (natural product mention)
4. Prove it works (numbers or testimonials)

## 13. Notes

- The writer agent drafts all posts for the week in a single batch to ensure
  voice consistency. Do not generate posts one at a time.
- Product posts should never read like ads. The product is mentioned in service
  of a story, insight, or solution — never as the subject of the post.
- When the human reviewer requests changes, the writer should redraft from
  scratch using the feedback, not just patch the existing text.
- Multilingual campaigns require native writing, not translation. Tone, humor,
  and cultural references don't translate directly.
- Track which post styles generate the most engagement over time and adjust
  the tone and framework preferences accordingly.
