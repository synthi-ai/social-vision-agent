# Engagement Booster Skill

## 1. Overview

The Engagement Booster skill transforms static social media posts into interactive,
algorithm-friendly content that drives measurable engagement. It applies platform-specific
CTA frameworks, engagement trigger patterns, and community building tactics to maximize
comments, shares, saves, and meaningful interactions.

This skill activates during the optimizer and writer nodes of the content graph. It sits
downstream of content creation and upstream of final approval, ensuring every post leaving
the pipeline has been engineered for maximum organic reach.

**Primary goal**: Turn passive readers into active participants.
**Secondary goal**: Signal relevance to platform algorithms through genuine engagement patterns.

## 2. Core Capabilities

- Apply platform-native CTA frameworks calibrated to each algorithm's ranking signals
- Classify and deploy 12 engagement trigger types (questions, polls, challenges, etc.)
- Distinguish authentic engagement patterns from engagement bait (and avoid the latter)
- Optimize post structure for comment velocity in the critical first 60 minutes
- Engineer share-driving and save-driving content patterns
- Provide engagement rate benchmarks per platform, post type, and industry vertical
- Recommend optimal posting cadence and reply strategies for sustained reach
- Build community flywheel effects through recurring content rituals
- Score posts against a 15-point engagement readiness checklist
- Map engagement signals to each platform's algorithm priorities

## 3. When to Use This Skill

Activate this skill when any of the following conditions are true:

- A post draft has been created and needs engagement optimization before publishing
- Engagement rates on recent posts have dropped below platform benchmarks
- The content strategy calls for community-building or audience growth
- A campaign goal includes specific engagement KPIs (comment count, share rate, save rate)
- The weekly brief specifies "engagement" or "community" as a campaign objective
- A post is being repurposed across platforms and needs CTA adaptation
- The optimizer node is processing any post in the content graph

## 4. Detailed Workflow

### Step 1: Engagement Audit of Draft Post

Read the draft post and score it against these five dimensions:

| Dimension         | Question to Ask                                    | Weight |
|-------------------|----------------------------------------------------|--------|
| Hook strength     | Does the first line create a reason to stop scrolling? | 25%    |
| Value clarity     | Is the takeaway obvious within 3 seconds?          | 20%    |
| Interaction prompt| Is there a clear, low-friction reason to engage?   | 25%    |
| Emotional trigger | Does the post evoke curiosity, surprise, or belonging? | 15%  |
| Algorithm fit     | Does the format match what the platform rewards?   | 15%    |

Score each dimension 1-5. Posts scoring below 18/25 need optimization.

### Step 2: Identify the Primary Engagement Goal

Each post should optimize for ONE primary engagement type:

| Goal      | What It Means                          | Best For                        |
|-----------|----------------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Comments  | Drive replies and conversation         | Authority building, algorithm boost |
| Shares    | Make people forward to their network   | Reach expansion, brand awareness |
| Saves     | Create reference-worthy content        | Long-term value, SEO signals    |
| Clicks    | Drive traffic to a link or profile     | Conversions, funnel entry       |
| Follows   | Convert viewers into followers         | Audience growth campaigns       |

### Step 3: Select Engagement Triggers

Choose 1-2 triggers from the Engagement Trigger Taxonomy (Section 6) that match
the post's goal and platform.

### Step 4: Rewrite or Augment the CTA

Replace generic CTAs with platform-specific, goal-aligned CTAs from the CTA
Frameworks section (Section 5).

### Step 5: Structure for Algorithm Signals

Restructure the post body to maximize the platform's specific engagement signals:
- LinkedIn: Optimize for dwell time (longer posts) and comment threads
- X: Optimize for retweets, quote tweets, and bookmark signals
- TikTok: Optimize for watch time, shares, and saves
- Facebook: Optimize for meaningful social interactions (MSI)

### Step 6: Add Community Building Elements

Layer in one community element from Section 11 (recurring rituals, audience callbacks,
insider language) to strengthen long-term engagement patterns.

### Step 7: Final Engagement Score

Re-score the optimized post. It must score 20/25 or higher to pass.

## 5. CTA Frameworks by Platform

### LinkedIn CTAs

**For Comments (highest algorithm weight on LinkedIn):**
- "What's your experience with [topic]? I'll reply to every comment today."
- "Agree or disagree? Drop your take below -- I'm genuinely curious."
- "I've seen 3 approaches to this. Which camp are you in? (A, B, or C)"
- "What would you add to this list? Best additions get featured in my next post."
- "Hot take or common sense? Tell me where you land."

**For Saves:**
- "Bookmark this for your next [specific situation]."
- "Save this framework -- you'll need it when [specific trigger event]."

**For Shares:**
- "Know someone building [specific thing]? Send this their way."
- "If this saved you 10 minutes, imagine what it does for your team. Share it."

**Avoid on LinkedIn:**
- "Like if you agree" (algorithm penalizes low-effort engagement bait since 2024)
- "Follow me for more" as the sole CTA (too generic)
- Polls with obvious/joke answers (engagement bait detection)

### X (Twitter) CTAs

**For Retweets:**
- "RT this so more [audience] see it. Took me [time] to figure out."
- "This thread took 8 hours to research. A retweet takes 1 second."

**For Replies:**
- "What's the most [superlative] [topic] you've encountered? Reply below."
- "Finishing this sentence: The biggest [topic] mistake is ___."
- "I'll mass-reply in 30 minutes. Drop your [topic] question."

**For Bookmarks:**
- "Bookmark this thread. You'll need it when [specific moment]."
- "Save > like. This one's a reference guide."

**For Follows:**
- "I post [topic] breakdowns like this every [frequency]. Follow to not miss them."
- Thread ender: "If this thread was useful, follow @handle for the next one on [topic]."

### TikTok CTAs

**For Comments:**
- "Comment [keyword] and I'll send you the full guide."
- "Which one are you? Type 1, 2, or 3."
- "Tell me your [topic] and I'll reply with advice."
- "Wrong answers only: what is [topic]?"

**For Shares:**
- "Send this to someone who [specific pain point]."
- "Your [friend/coworker/boss] needs to see this."

**For Saves:**
- "Save this for when you [specific future moment]."
- "You'll forget this in 10 minutes. Save it."

**For Follows:**
- "Part 2 drops [day]. Follow so you don't miss it."
- "I make [content type] every [frequency]. Hit follow."

### Facebook CTAs

**For Comments:**
- "Which of these resonates most? Comment your number."
- "Has this happened to you? Tell us your story."
- "What's missing from this list? Help me improve it."
- "Finish this sentence: If I could change one thing about [topic], it would be ___."

**For Shares:**
- "Share this with someone who's dealing with [specific challenge]."
- "Tag a friend who needs this reminder."
- "Your [role] friends will thank you for sharing this."

**For Reactions:**
- Use the reaction framework: Ask questions where different reactions = different answers
- "Love if you agree, Wow if this surprised you, Haha if you've been there"

## 9. Quality Checklist

Before publishing, verify every item:

- [ ] Post has a clear, single primary engagement goal (comment/share/save/click/follow)
- [ ] CTA is platform-native and matches the engagement goal
- [ ] CTA requires low cognitive effort (easy to respond to)
- [ ] Post uses at least one engagement trigger from the taxonomy
- [ ] First line is a scroll-stopper (under 100 characters, pattern interrupt)
- [ ] Post body delivers enough value to justify the CTA
- [ ] No engagement bait red flags (see Section 12)
- [ ] Post structure matches platform algorithm priorities
- [ ] Reply strategy is planned (who replies, how fast, what tone)
- [ ] Post avoids external links in the main body (LinkedIn, X, Facebook)
- [ ] Emotional trigger is present (curiosity, surprise, belonging, aspiration)
- [ ] Post length matches platform sweet spot
- [ ] Community element is included (callback, ritual, insider reference)
- [ ] Timing is aligned with audience peak hours
- [ ] Hashtags follow platform-specific best practices

## 12. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Better Approach |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| "Please like and share!" | Algorithms detect and suppress explicit engagement begging. Audience perceives it as desperate. | Create content so valuable people share without being asked, or use subtle CTAs tied to value ("Share this with a friend who's struggling with X"). |
| Asking questions you don't care about | Audience can tell when questions are performative. Kills trust and future engagement. | Only ask questions you'll genuinely engage with in the replies. If you won't reply, don't ask. |
| Same CTA on every post | Audience develops CTA blindness. Engagement drops 40-60% after 5 identical CTAs. | Rotate between comment, share, save, and follow CTAs. Use different trigger types weekly. |
| Ignoring comments for hours | Comment velocity in the first 60 minutes determines algorithmic reach. Late replies signal low relevance. | Reply to every comment within 2 hours. Aim for 5+ replies in the first 30 minutes. Prioritize substantive replies. |
| Generic one-word replies | "Thanks!" and emoji-only replies don't count as meaningful interactions. Algorithms discount them. | Reply with 2+ sentences that add value, ask a follow-up question, or acknowledge the commenter's specific point. |
| Cross-posting identical content | Each platform has different norms, formats, and algorithm preferences. Identical posts underperform by 50-70%. | Adapt the core message to each platform's CTA framework, length, and engagement patterns. |
| Asking overly broad questions | "What do you think about AI?" is too vague. Nobody feels compelled to answer. | Narrow the scope: "What's ONE AI tool you use daily that most people don't know about?" Specificity drives responses. |
| Front-loading the CTA | Asking for engagement before delivering value feels transactional. | Deliver value first, then earn the right to ask for engagement at the end. |
| Using engagement bait patterns | LinkedIn, X, and Facebook actively detect and suppress "Like if you...", "Raise your hand if...", poll-style posts with joke answers. | Use authentic engagement triggers that create genuine conversation. |
| Posting without a reply plan | Publishing content and walking away wastes the critical first-hour algorithm window. | Schedule 30-minute reply blocks immediately after posting. Brief your team on reply tone and strategy. |

## 13. Advanced Techniques

### Comment Thread Seeding

Post your content, then immediately add 2-3 strategic comments yourself:
1. **The resource comment**: Drop the link or additional resource here
2. **The counterpoint comment**: Present the other side of your argument to spark debate
3. **The question deepener**: Ask a more specific follow-up to your main post

This creates the appearance of an active comment section, lowering the barrier for
others to join.

### The Engagement Ladder

Structure your content calendar to progressively increase engagement difficulty:

| Week | Engagement Ask Level | Example |
|------|---------------------|---------|
| 1    | Passive (likes)     | Share a valuable insight, no CTA required |
| 2    | Low-effort (saves)  | "Save this checklist for later" |
| 3    | Medium-effort (comments) | "Which of these 3 do you use?" |
| 4    | High-effort (create) | "Share your version of this framework" |

This trains your audience to engage progressively rather than asking too much too soon.

### Reciprocity Loops

Spend 15 minutes engaging with your target audience's content BEFORE you post.
Like, comment thoughtfully, share. When you publish 30 minutes later, those people
are primed to reciprocate. This is not manipulation -- it's being a genuine participant
in the community.

### The Controversy Gradient

Not all controversial takes are equal. Use this gradient:

| Level | Type | Risk | Engagement Multiplier |
|-------|------|------|-----------------------|
| 1 | Mild preference ("I prefer X over Y") | None | 1.5x |
| 2 | Industry contrarian ("Framework X is overrated") | Low | 2.5x |
| 3 | Challenging norms ("College degrees don't matter for this") | Medium | 4x |
| 4 | Values-adjacent ("The industry has a [problem] problem") | High | 6x+ |
| 5 | Directly political or personal attacks | Extreme | Do not use |

Stay between levels 1-3 for consistent, sustainable engagement growth.
Level 4 should be used sparingly and only when you have genuine conviction.

### Engagement Windows Strategy

Optimize not just when you post, but when you engage:

| Platform  | Best Post Time (UTC) | Critical Reply Window | Engagement Decay |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------------------|------------------|
| LinkedIn  | 07:00-09:00 Tue-Thu | First 60 minutes      | 80% after 8 hours |
| X         | 12:00-15:00 Mon-Fri | First 30 minutes      | 90% after 4 hours |
| TikTok    | 18:00-21:00 any day | First 120 minutes     | 70% after 24 hours |
| Facebook  | 09:00-11:00 Wed-Fri | First 90 minutes      | 85% after 12 hours |

### Community Rituals

Create recurring engagement patterns that train your audience to participate:

- **Weekly series**: "Tool Tuesday", "Failure Friday", "Weekend Challenge"
- **Recurring prompts**: Same question format monthly with different topics
- **Milestone celebrations**: Engage the community in growth markers
- **Audience spotlights**: Feature top commenters or best responses
- **Running inside jokes**: References that reward long-time followers

## 14. Engagement Bait vs. Authentic Engagement

### Red Flags (Platforms Penalize These)

- "Like if you agree, comment if you disagree"
- "I bet this won't get 100 likes"
- Polls with one obviously correct answer and joke alternatives
- "Who else?" with a universally relatable statement
- Reaction-fishing with emotional manipulation
- Share-gating (hiding content behind share requirements)
- "Only 1% can solve this" puzzles with obvious answers

### Green Flags (Platforms Reward These)

- Genuine questions the author cares about and replies to
- Sharing specific experiences and inviting similar stories
- Asking for expert input on a real decision you're making
- Creating content so useful people save/share organically
- Building on trending conversations with original insight
- Inviting constructive disagreement on professional topics
- Celebrating community members' achievements

### The Test

Before adding any engagement element, ask: "Would I engage with this if
someone else posted it?" If the answer is no, it's bait. Remove it.

## 15. Settings and Configuration

The engagement-booster skill can be configured via the weekly brief or per-post overrides:

```yaml
# In config/weekly_brief.yaml
engagement_settings:
  default_engagement_goal: "comments"    # comments | shares | saves | clicks | follows
  min_engagement_score: 20               # Minimum score out of 25 to pass optimization
  reply_window_minutes: 120              # Maximum time before all comments get replies
  first_reply_minutes: 5                 # Target time for first reply after posting
  cta_rotation: true                     # Rotate CTA types across posts in a batch
  engagement_bait_filter: strict         # strict | moderate | off
  community_ritual: "Tool Tuesday"       # Active recurring series name
  controversy_max_level: 3               # Maximum controversy gradient level (1-5)
```

Per-post override in campaign config:

```yaml
campaigns:
  - name: "Product Launch"
    engagement_goal: "shares"            # Override default for this campaign
    min_engagement_score: 22             # Higher bar for launch content
    controversy_max_level: 2             # Play it safe during launches
```

## 16. Notes

- Engagement optimization should never compromise authenticity. A lower-engagement
  authentic post outperforms a higher-engagement inauthentic one long-term.
- Algorithm signals change frequently. Review platform documentation quarterly and
  update the signal tables in Section 7.
- Engagement rate benchmarks in Section 8 are based on 2025-2026 data for B2B tech
  accounts with 1K-50K followers. Adjust expectations for different verticals and
  account sizes.
- The optimizer node should apply this skill AFTER the writer node has created the
  core content. Never sacrifice message clarity for engagement optimization.
- When engagement_bait_filter is set to "strict", the skill will reject any post
  containing patterns from the Red Flags list in Section 14, even if the engagement
  score is passing.
- Reply strategy is as important as post content. Budget equal time for replies as
  for post creation.
- Track engagement rates weekly and compare against the benchmarks in Section 8.
  Adjust trigger selection and CTA frameworks based on what performs for your
  specific audience.
